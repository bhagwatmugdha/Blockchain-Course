In very simple terms, a blockchain is nothing but a chain of blocks that contains information. While this technology was originally designed by a group of researchers back in the 90s, it was first used extensively by Satoshi Nakamoto to create the most popular cryptocurrency- Bitcoin.

IBM formally defines blockchain as a shared, distributed ledger that facilitates the process of recording transactions and tracking assets in a network. A blockchain is often described as tamper resistant and tamper evident. But how does a blockchain achieve this?
